Topics are active agents wagering their resources—their **Stakes**—to influence the outcome. To rigorously quantify the causal weight ($\phi$) of a specific Topic ($T_i$) in the consensus, the **Shapley Value** from cooperative game theory is applied.

The Shapley Value $\phi_i(v)$ represents the marginal causal weight a Topic contributes to a collision:

$$\mathbf{\phi_i(v)} = \sum_{S \subseteq N \setminus \{i\}} \frac{|S|! (|N|-|S|-1)!}{|N|!} [v(S \cup \{i\}) - v(S)]$$

Where:
* $\phi_i(v)$: The causal weight (Stake) of Topic $T_i$ in the event.
* $N$: The total set of Topics participating in the collision.
* $S$: A subset of Topics (a partial context).
* $v(S)$: The Characteristic Function—the state of reality created by coalition $S$.
* $[v(S \cup \{i\}) - v(S)]$: The **Marginal Contribution**, which is the difference in reality when Topic $T_i$ is added to context $S$.

#### The Consensus Function (The Ledger Entry)

The negotiation is concluded by summing these weighted inputs to produce the final Event recorded on the Ledger:

$$\mathbf{Event_{consensus}} = \sum_{i \in N} \phi_i \times C_i$$

Where $C_i$ is the specific context or intent provided by Topic $i$.
