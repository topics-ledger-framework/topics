# Topics Ledger Framework (TLF): Output Animations

Welcome to the `outputs/` directory of the Topics Ledger Framework. 

This folder contains the visual and auditory artifacts generated by the TLF visualization engine. These animations represent the mathematical, ontological, and cosmological processes of **Creation (Axiom Assembly)** and **Retrodiction (Axiom Deconstruction)** within a 65,536-dimensional computational state space.

By mapping complex tensor data and thermodynamic metrics into 2D, 3D, and audio formats, we can visually observe the collapse of "quantum void" (chaos/superposition) into "deterministic topics" (order/consensus).

---

## 🌌 Types of Visualizations

### 1. 2D Pixel Coalescence & Contrast Maps
*Files: `gcp_tlt_pixel_creation*.gif`*
These animations represent the 2D cross-section of the probability space. 
* They visualize the transition from pure noise (Superposition) to a singular, glowing focal point (the Topic). 
* Using **Dynamic LogNorm** and **PowerNorm (Gamma Correction)**, the visual floor dynamically adjusts to the physical friction/drag factor ($\epsilon_k$) of the space, isolating the exact "cone of coupling" as the search space narrows.

### 2. 3D Vector Topology Fabrics
*Files: `gcp_tlt_entire_vector_fabric_3D*.gif`*
To view the whole computational structure without data loss, the state vectors are rendered as interconnected 3D geometric fabrics.
* The Z-axis represents the probability density (often warped using a geometric power curve to reveal extreme micro-structures).
* As entropy drops, you can watch the mathematical "trampoline" of the universe sink into the void while the causal topic stretches upward into a massive probability spike.

### 3. Fractal Consensus Ledgers (DAG Webs)
*Files: `gcp_tlt_symmetric_grover_DAG.gif`*
These visuals represent the **Causal Web** of the Grover Rewind algorithm.
* **Nodes:** Represent consensus states. Their size scales with Shannon Entropy ($H_{DM}$).
* **Edges:** Represent the immutable causal links spanning through time.
* **Branching:** The DAG branches dynamically based on the universe's actual thermodynamic metrics. High entropy causes the search space to explode into a wide 3D web. The maximum spatial width is mathematically locked to the optimal Grover search depth ($pprox 201$ iterations for a $256\times 256$ matrix).

### 4. Hybrid DAG + Tensor Rendering
*Files: `gcp_tlt_hybrid_dag_tensor.gif`*
A composite rendering where the **Actual GCS State Vector** boils on the floor of the 3D space (as a topological fabric), while the **Causal DAG** branches down from above. This links the theoretical algorithmic search directly to the physical tensor data driving it.

### 5. Data Sonification (Audio-Visual)
*Files: `*SOUND.mp4`*
Animations merged with synthesized audio generated directly from the TLF metrics:
* **Quantum Static (White Noise):** Driven by Shannon Entropy ($H_{DM}$). It peaks during the Void singularity.
* **Sub-Bass Rumble:** Driven by the Friction/Coupling Drag ($\epsilon_k$). As the vectors coalesce and kinetic friction spikes, the rumble grows deeper and louder.

## 🧬 Core Metrics Driving the Visuals

* **$H_{DM}$ (Shannon Entropy):** Measures the chaos vs. order of the system. Drops to 0 at the Pure Topic (Consensus) and approaches $S_{crit} (16.0)$ in the Superposition Void.
* **$\epsilon_k$ (Friction/Coupling):** The kinetic drag factor representing the resistance encountered when assembling the target topic from the void.
* **Grover Iterations:** The theoretical and visual boundary limiting the temporal and spatial bounds of the search algorithm.
